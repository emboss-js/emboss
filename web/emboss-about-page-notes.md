# Emboss — About Page Notes

## The Core Insight

Gloria spent her nursing career taking complex topics — medical diagnoses — and breaking them down into smaller, more manageable, easier-to-understand pieces for people going through scary and painful things. That's as much art as it is science.

That's the same thing Emboss does. Takes a powerful but raw engine and makes it legible, approachable, usable by the person sitting in front of it.

## The Analogy

A nurse doesn't hand a patient a stack of lab results and say "figure it out." You translate complexity into clarity at the exact moment someone is most overwhelmed.

Project management is one of those overwhelming moments for a lot of teams. Deadlines, dependencies, scope — it's the same overwhelm. The tool should reduce that, not add to it. The surface is where the anxiety either calms down or ramps up.

## Page Structure

Don't open with credentials. Open with the insight:

> Breaking down complexity for people in hard moments is what I've always done. First at the bedside. Now in the interface.

Then the nursing background and cognitive diversity research aren't résumé lines — they're evidence for a claim you've already made.

## Credibility Elements (secondary, not the lead)

- RN background — clinical experience translating complexity
- Study of neurodivergent and cognitive diversity — understanding how different brains process interfaces
- Leantime — the product that came from this perspective
- Emboss — the design philosophy made into a library

## Positioning

This is a point of view nobody else in the Gantt chart / PM tool space can claim. It's not a marketing angle — it's the actual reason this work exists.

## Tone Guidance

- "Confident craft, not corporate polish"
- Self-aware, a little dry, never desperate
- Honor what came before (Frappe, open source community)
- The world of free software self-selects for people who care — they don't need convincing that design matters, they need to know someone credible is behind it
