Emboss — Revised Feature Model

Tiptap-style extension architecture. Core is minimal. Everything else is opt-in extensions — some free, some paid.

Key lesson from Tiptap: They started by selling individual Pro extensions. Realized "these smaller pieces weren't where our value was. They were common needs, but not really our moat." They open-sourced 10 formerly paid extensions and shifted to selling complete feature bundles (collaboration, AI, documents) that solve whole problems.

For Emboss: The core is the redesigned Frappe engine with grayscale + dark themes. Everything else — sidebar, phases, milestones, tooltips, assignees — is an extension you import separately. Free extensions ship in the same package. Paid extensions require a license. The developer chooses what to install. Nothing is bundled by default except the bars.

🤔 The Gray Zone — Could Go Either Way

These are NOT in Frappe. They're real new work. The question: which ones build enough trust to justify giving away, and which ones are clearly "you're paying for our thinking"?

Sidebar
Case for free: Without it, Emboss is just bars with labels on them (like Frappe). The sidebar makes it feel like a real tool. Adoption driver.
Case for paid: It's significant new UI work. Three states (full/rail/hidden), resize, transitions. Not in Frappe at all.
Phase grouping
Case for free: Flat task lists are unusable past 10 items. Phases make Emboss viable for real projects. People won't adopt without this.
Case for paid: It's a complete data model addition. Collapsible rows, derived progress, color system. Frappe is flat.
Inline name editing
Case for free: Basic quality-of-life. Click to edit text is expected in 2026.
Case for paid: Frappe has no editing of names at all. This is new functionality with keyboard handling, phase-colored borders, save/cancel.
Milestones (as first-class type)
Case for free: Frappe has milestones (via custom_class). We're improving them with diamond shapes and progress fill. Could argue it's a redesign of existing.
Case for paid: Our milestones are fundamentally different — distinct data type with progress fill, phase coloring. Much more than a CSS class.

Proposed Split

Here's one way to draw the line. Open for discussion.

Option A — Generous free (more Tiptap-like)

Free extensions: Today marker, dependency dots, hover tooltips, sidebar (basic — full + hidden only, no rail), milestones (diamond shape, no progress fill)

Paid extensions: Phase grouping + collapsing, inline editing, sidebar rail mode + resize, milestone progress fill, assignees, subtasks, critical path, export, swimlanes, baseline

Paid templates: Vivid, Presentation, Dense, Minimal

Logic: Free gets you a beautiful Gantt with basic sidebar and diamonds. Paid unlocks the organizational layer (phases) and the polish details (milestone fill, rail mode, editing).

Option B — Strategic free (tighter)

Free extensions: Today marker, dependency dots, hover tooltips

Paid extensions: Sidebar (all modes), phase grouping + collapsing, milestones (full), inline editing, assignees, subtasks, critical path, export, swimlanes, baseline

Paid templates: Vivid, Presentation, Dense, Minimal

Logic: Free core is already dramatically better than Frappe — beautiful grayscale bars with smart arrows and tooltips. Everything structural (sidebar, phases, milestones) is paid. More revenue potential but smaller free tier.

Option C — Somewhere in between

Free extensions: Today marker, dependency dots, hover tooltips, sidebar (full + hidden), phase grouping (basic — group headers, no collapsing)

Paid extensions: Phase collapsing, sidebar rail + resize, milestones (full with progress), inline editing, assignees, subtasks, critical path, export, swimlanes, baseline

Paid templates: Vivid, Presentation, Dense, Minimal

Logic: Free gives you organized tasks with a sidebar — usable for real projects. Paid adds the polish and power features. Phases exist free but collapsing is paid. Sidebar works but doesn't have the fancy rail mode.

Tiptap's lesson for us: They originally sold individual extensions and realized it wasn't their moat. They shifted to selling complete feature bundles that solve whole problems.

For Emboss, this might mean: don't sell sidebar for $49 and phases for $49 separately. Instead, sell bundles:

• Organization Bundle ($79): Sidebar (all modes) + phase grouping + collapsing + milestones + inline editing
• People Bundle ($49): Assignees + swimlane/resource view
• Analysis Bundle ($79): Critical path + baseline comparison + export
• Subtask Pack ($49): Nesting + collapsible children
• Design Templates ($49 each / $99 all): Vivid, Presentation, Dense, Minimal
• Pro Bundle ($199): Everything

This way people buy solutions, not features.
