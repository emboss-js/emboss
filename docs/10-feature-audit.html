Emboss — Feature Audit

What comes from Frappe MIT (can't sell), what's new design work (free), and what's genuinely new functionality (premium).

Principle: Frappe is MIT. Anything that exists in Frappe v1 — even if we redesign it — ships free. We are not selling Frappe's work. We are selling new features that don't exist in Frappe, and complete design systems (premium templates) that represent original design work.

New Not in Frappe — New in Emboss, Ships Free

These features do NOT exist in Frappe v1. They're new Emboss work. We're giving them away free because they make the core experience good enough to drive adoption.

FeatureWhy It's Free
Sidebar Frappe has no sidebar at all — labels sit on bars. The sidebar (task list panel) is fundamental to usability. Three states: full/rail/hidden. This is core UX, not a luxury.
Phase grouping Frappe is a flat task list. Grouping tasks into phases (collapsible rows with derived progress) is basic organization. Without it the chart is just a flat list of bars.
Collapsible phases Goes with phase grouping. Chevron to expand/collapse. Essential for any project with more than 10 tasks.
Done/active/upcoming status styling Frappe treats all bars the same visually. Status-based opacity/styling hierarchy is the core design insight of Emboss. In grayscale: done = faded, active = full, upcoming = lighter. This is the design thinking people are adopting Emboss for.
Today marker (visual line) Frappe has a button to scroll to today. The visual line with pulsing dot is new. Standard in most serious Gantt tools.
Dependency dots on bars New. Small dots on bar edges that signal "this has dependencies — hover to see." Solves the discoverability problem with hover-only arrows.
Inline name editing Frappe has no way to edit task names in the chart. Click-to-edit with phase-colored border is new.
Grayscale theme (light) The default beautiful design system. This is Emboss's free identity — the thoughtful grayscale with glass treatment, spacing, transitions.
Dark mode theme Frappe has no dark mode. Two free themes (light grayscale + dark) covers the two most common preferences.

The Honest Question

Are we actually selling enough new stuff?

The free tier includes everything Frappe has (redesigned) plus sidebar, phases, collapsing, status styling, today marker, dependency dots, inline editing, grayscale + dark themes. That's a LOT of free.

Premium is: 4 color/design templates + 6 feature components.

Templates ($49 each, $99 all): Vivid, Presentation, Dense, Minimal
Components ($99 each, $149 all): Assignees, Subtasks, Critical Path, Export, Swimlanes, Baseline
Pro Bundle ($199): Everything

The question: is the design thinking in the free tier so good that people don't need premium? Or does the free tier make people trust the quality enough to buy premium for their specific needs?

I think it's the latter. The free tier is the marketing. The templates are for teams who want polished visuals without doing design work. The components are for teams who need specific PM features.
